Inner ring (12 states): duodecimal digit

This is a mixed-radix counter: 60 = 5 × 12. For each step of the inner ring (0→↋), the outer ring stays. After inner wraps, the outer advances by 1.
Examples
0 → (0, 0)  •  11 → (0, {{ digit12(11) }})

Why 12×5?
12 has divisors 2,3,4,6 → clean fractions. 5 keeps the outer wheel compact. Together they yield 60 states—the same structure behind minutes/seconds and degrees.
Tip: toggle duodecimal glyphs to see how 10, 11 become ↊, ↋. The arc lengths are exact: inner 30° per segment (12 parts), outer 72° per segment (5 parts).
